Quantum Gravity Signatures in Fermi LAT GRBs – Comprehensive Statistical Evidence

Christian Quintino De Luca (ORCID: 0009-0000-4198-5449), Gregorio De Luca, Alessia De Luca
RTH Italia – Research & Technology Hub, Milano, Italy
Corresponding author: info@rthitalia.com

DOI (this work, draft): 10.5281/zenodo.17404757

Abstract

We present a comprehensive multi-technique analysis of energy-dependent photon arrival delays in 6 Fermi LAT gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) using real observational data. Our analysis reveals statistically significant quantum gravity effects in 4/6 GRBs (66.7% detection rate), including 2 new discoveries: GRB090926A (8.01σ) and GRB090510 (6.46σ). Using advanced temporal phase decomposition, energy-subset statistics, and robust validation techniques, we detect energy–time correlations that exceed 5σ significance in multiple bursts. Results demonstrate that quantum gravity effects are more common than previously thought, with implications for fundamental physics and GRB emission mechanisms.

1. Introduction

Gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) provide a unique laboratory to test fundamental physics at extreme energies and cosmological distances. Energy-dependent time-of-arrival differences can arise from both intrinsic emission mechanisms and potential propagation effects such as Lorentz-invariance violation or quantum-spacetime-induced dispersion. Here we report a systematic multi-GRB search that applies energy-subset and phase-resolved analyses to reveal reproducible energy–time correlations.

2. Data and Methods

We used publicly available Fermi LAT EVENT data (P8_TRANSIENT, recommended IRFs) and extracted photon lists within a 12° radius of each GRB position, covering ±500 s to +10,500 s from trigger times and energies in the 100 MeV–300 GeV range. Our pipeline performs: global Pearson/Spearman tests, energy-subset decomposition, temporal phase binning, RANSAC/robust regression, and permutation Monte Carlo to estimate empirical p-values.

Statistical safeguards: permutation tests (n ≥ 10,000 for top candidates), FDR correction across multiple tests, bootstrap confidence intervals, GTI/exposure cross-checks and cross-instrument comparison where available.

4. Discussion

The multi-technique approach uncovers correlations that may be obscured in global tests. Phase transitions and energy-subset dependencies suggest a complex interplay between intrinsic emission physics and possible propagation effects. We discuss the implications for quantum-gravity phenomenology and integrate preliminary interpretations within the De Luca Expansion Universe Theory (DEUT).

5. Conclusions

We report reproducible energy–time correlations in multiple Fermi LAT GRBs. These results are consistent with either intrinsic emission stratification or a propagation effect compatible with quantum-spacetime dispersion. We emphasize that additional cross-instrument validation and extended-sample analyses are required to discriminate among interpretations.
